Project Overview

Atapuerca is home to a remarkable group of archaeological sites that have been key to understanding human evolution in Europe. In 1994, a new species —Homo antecessor— was discovered here, pushing back the timeline for the first human presence on the continent. Two decades later, the discovery of a new human face at Sima del Elefante extended this timeline even further, revealing evidence of hominins dating back at least 1.4 million years. I was fortunate to complete my PhD within the framework of this extraordinary project. Specifically, I investigated the bioenergetic costs of survival for these early hominin species, using a combination of calorimetry measurements and computational modeling.
